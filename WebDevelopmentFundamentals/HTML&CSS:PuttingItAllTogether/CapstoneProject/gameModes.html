Unrated

Unrated is a game mode in Valorant that allows players to play in a more casual setting without the pressure of losing rank points. In this mode, players can still compete and play with other players, but the outcome of the match doesn't affect their rank.

Unrated is often used as a warm-up game mode or a way for players to practice their skills in a more relaxed environment. It is also a great way for new players to get used to the mechanics of the game and learn the different maps and agents.

The rules of the game are the same as the competitive mode, with the exception that there are no ranks and the games do not affect the player's MMR (Matchmaking Rating) or Elo. Players can still communicate and strategize with their team, but the game is generally less serious and more laid-back compared to competitive mode.

Competitive

The Competitive game mode in Valorant is a 5v5 mode that involves two teams, the attacking team, and the defending team. The attacking team's objective is to plant a bomb (known as the Spike) at one of the designated sites on the map and defend it until it explodes. The defending team's objective is to prevent the attacking team from planting the Spike or defuse it if it's already planted. The first team to win 13 rounds out of a maximum of 24 rounds wins the game.

The Competitive mode also includes a ranked system where players are placed into ranks based on their performance in previous matches. The ranking system uses a rating called the "Elo system," which adjusts based on wins, losses, and personal performance. The more a player wins, the higher their rank becomes. Winning matches against higher-ranked teams will result in more rank increases than winning against lower-ranked teams.

To participate in the Competitive mode, players must reach level 5 and complete a set of placement matches to determine their initial rank. Players are then matched with others of similar skill level. The Competitive mode is considered the most challenging game mode in Valorant and is where players can test their skills against other highly skilled players.

Swift play

Swiftplay is a best-of-nine spike (Plant/Defuse) mode. It has most of the same gameplay rules as Unrated but with the following changes to adjust for its shorter duration:

  • All player start each half with two points for their ultimate.
  • No credit bonuses for loss streaks.
  • Credits earned based on round outcome have adjusted values and are the same for both teams, with the exception of round 2.

Spike Rush

Spike Rush is a fast-paced, casual game mode in Valorant. In Spike Rush, each team consists of five players and the objective is to plant or defuse the Spike, just like in the standard game mode. However, there are several key differences.

Firstly, Spike Rush matches are much shorter than standard matches, usually lasting around 10 minutes. Secondly, every player starts the match with the same weapon, and powerful abilities and weapons are randomly given throughout the match, which changes every round. This makes for a more chaotic and unpredictable game experience.

Another difference is that the Spike explodes much faster in Spike Rush, usually taking only seven seconds to detonate after it is planted. This makes the gameplay more fast-paced and intense, as players have less time to react and defuse the Spike.

Overall, Spike Rush is a fun and fast-paced game mode that offers a different type of gameplay experience compared to the more serious and competitive standard and ranked game modes in Valorant.

Deathmatch

Deathmatch is a game mode in Valorant where players compete against each other in a free-for-all battle. The objective of this mode is to score as many kills as possible within a set time limit. Unlike other modes, there is no spike to plant or defuse, and no teams to work with. Players are able to respawn immediately after they die and are provided with unlimited money to purchase weapons and abilities. The first player to reach a set amount of kills, or the player with the most kills at the end of the time limit, is declared the winner. This mode is often used for warm-ups or for players to practice their aim and improve their skills.

Escalation

Escalation is a game mode in Valorant where two teams compete to level up their weapons through a series of progressively more challenging rounds. At the beginning of each round, all players on both teams are given the same randomly selected weapon, and the goal is to score a certain number of kills with it to progress to the next level.

As players progress, the weapons they receive become increasingly powerful and challenging to use, adding an extra layer of difficulty and excitement to the game. The first team to reach the final level and get a kill with the designated golden gun wins the match.

Escalation is a fast-paced and fun game mode that allows players to experiment with different weapons and practice their aim in a competitive setting. It's a great way for new players to learn the game and for experienced players to test their skills in a fresh and exciting way.

Replication

Replication is a limited-time game mode in Valorant that was introduced in patch 3.05. In this game mode, each player on the team will play with the same randomly selected agent, with the agents changing every round. This means that both teams will have the same agents in each round, making it a test of skill and strategy with each team starting on an even playing field.

In Replication, all abilities have increased charges, which makes for a more chaotic and fast-paced gameplay experience. This game mode also has a unique twist in that each player can earn a "replicant ult point" by getting a kill, which can be used to activate their ultimate ability.

Replication has three rounds with the team that wins two of those rounds emerging victorious. The first team to reach 13 round wins will be declared the winner.

Snowball Fight

Snowball Fight is a 5v5 team deathmatch-style mode where the first team to 50 kills wins. All players load into game with an agent of their choice and only have access to a Snowball Launcher. This weapon fires projectile-based snowballs that have a travel time and an arc. The Snowball Launcher has infinite ammo but can only fire one snowball at a time with a small reload in between shots. Snowballs deal 150 damage upon coming into contact with an enemy player.

Snowball Fight grants 750 XP for each game completed, and 150 bonus XP for the winning team, but cannot be used to progress missions.
