Valorant Infomation

Introduction

Valorant is a competitive strategy first person shooter, where you strategize team synergy and agent compatibility with maps with your team against other players. Valorant requires patience, precision, and communication. If you are new to the game of Valorant you may want to stick around. This website provides information on the different types of agents and their roles, the different maps that are in the game, and the different types of game modes. Use this information to your advantage and learn as much as you can before throwing yourself into the game!

Introduction to the Agents

There are multiple agents in Valorant and they are separated into 4 different roles. The 4 roles designated roles are: Initiator, Duelist, Controller, and Sentinel. Each role serves a general function, each character that fall in a specific role does the specified responsibilities in different ways. Click the Agent tab to learn more about the agents and their roles.

Introduction to the Maps

Just like there are multiple agents in Valorant there are also multiple maps. There are 9 maps in valorant: Bind, Split, Haven, Icebox, Pearl, Fracture, Breeze, Ascent, and Lotus. Each map is unique and different from the others. The primary objective of having multiple maps is to facilitate variable creation from one game to the next. The maps will go on rotation with the start of a new season. Each map has a handful of agents that can optimally use their utility. While some agents are good and played more often on some maps, they can also be picked less on other maps.

Introduction to the Game Modes

Valorant also contains multiple game modes. There's unrated for the casual players that just want to relax and enjoy the gameplay. Then there's a competitive where you face other competitive players and wager your rank rating and climb the rank ladder to achieve the best rank you can. There are other game modes too for the more casual players. There's swiftplay and spike rush for those who are on a time crunch and can't play a full game. Deathmatch is a free-for-all, perfect for competitive players warming up for their games. From time to time Valorant will also include a limited time game modes such as: Replication, Snowball fight, and Escalation.
